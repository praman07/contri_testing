# Final Vision

Override's long-term destination is a trusted cognitive runtime that understands the user's digital environment, collaborates through context rather than commands, and safely coordinates complex work across software ecosystems.

## Five-year vision

In five years, Override should reliably understand active projects, browser research, documents, terminals, meetings, goals, and previous sessions. It should plan multi-step tasks, execute through governed providers, verify outcomes, and remember lessons. It should support coding, research, learning, meetings, career workflows, diagrams, and desktop/browser automation.

## Ten-year vision

In ten years, Override should be a mature local-first operating layer for knowledge work. It should maintain a rich private world model, coordinate specialized agents, adapt interfaces per task, support multiple projects and devices, and preserve user agency. It should survive model, OS, UI, and provider changes.

## Major capabilities

- Continuous observation with permission.
- Persistent world model.
- Workspace intelligence.
- Session recall.
- Long-term goals and memory.
- Reasoning and planning.
- Provider-based execution.
- Verification and recovery.
- Adaptive UI.
- Local-first privacy and safety.

## Success criteria

Override succeeds when users think in outcomes rather than app operations, trust what Override recommends, understand what it is doing, and can safely resume complex work after interruption.
